Future Opportunities

The future of MIKE‑CORE: new ecosystemic possibilities, social impact, anti‑waste initiatives and scalable training formats for 2026–2030.

This page describes only the public future vision.
Why Future Opportunities?

MIKE‑CORE grows as an ecosystem — not through speed, but through stability, reproducibility and socio‑technical value. This page shows where MIKE‑CORE can evolve without losing its core principles.

Focus
Ecosystemic growth

Small projects become larger ecosystems that protect people, hardware and knowledge.

Goal
A future without throw‑away thinking

Technology, people and knowledge are used sustainably — never discarded.

Approach
Reproducible models

What works is documented and applied again in other contexts.

New Ecosystemic Opportunities

In the coming years, MIKE‑CORE can expand into new domains, collaborations and socio‑technical projects.

Opportunity 1
MIKE‑CORE‑F Labs

Small physical spaces where youth repair hardware, learn ecosystemic thinking and run anti‑waste projects.

Opportunity 2
Digital Ecosystem Academy

Online modules, visual maps and reproducible exercises for anyone who wants to enter the ecosystem.

Opportunity 3
Community Repair Networks

Local networks that rescue hardware, share knowledge and build ecosystemic stability.

Future Social Impact

MIKE‑CORE can play a major role in digital inclusion, anti‑waste culture and ecosystemic coaching.

Impact 1
Inclusion Programs

New pathways for youth, job seekers and vulnerable groups.

Impact 2
Anti‑Waste Initiatives

Larger hardware rescue projects with schools, companies and local authorities.

Impact 3
Ecosystemic Coaching

Professional guidance around drift, noise and stability in organisations.

Roadmap 2026–2030

The future of MIKE‑CORE follows a reproducible cycle of growth.

Phase 1 — Consolidation
Strengthening stability

Expanding public layers, refining documentation, reinforcing ecosystemic language.

Phase 2 — Expansion
New projects

Labs, repair networks, digital academy, inclusion pathways.

Phase 3 — Scalability
Reproducible models

Projects become ecosystemic blueprints that work again elsewhere.

Phase 4 — Ecosystem Growth
Regional impact

Collaborations with cities, schools, organisations and local initiatives.
