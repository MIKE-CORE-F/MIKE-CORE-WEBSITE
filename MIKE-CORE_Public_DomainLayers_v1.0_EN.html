Public 3‑Layer Domain View

This page introduces the public 3‑layer domain structure of MIKE‑CORE: the ecosystem foundation layer, the conceptual domain layer, and the module‑and‑flow layer. The internal blueprint, validation chains and domain logic remain intentionally closed.

Why a 3‑Layer Domain Structure?
Public Architecture

MIKE‑CORE is described as an architectural layer that sits above frameworks and safeguards the stability of the whole. Frameworks are tools. Ecosystems are foundations. The 3‑layer domain view makes this visible without exposing internal mechanics.

  • Layer 1 – Ecosystem Foundation: principles, axes, stability frameworks
  • Layer 2 – Conceptual Domains: thematic ecosystem areas
  • Layer 3 – Modules & Flows: public modules, checks and ecosystem flows
Layer 1 – Ecosystem Foundation
Identity • Direction • Transition

The foundation layer structures everything beneath the surface. It contains the ecosystem principles, the I‑, R‑ and T‑axis, and the stability and reproducibility frameworks.

1.1
Ecosystem Principles

These principles define the natural laws of the ecosystem: stability over rhythm, coherence over speed, and reproducibility over improvisation.

1.2
Axes (I‑, R‑, T‑Axis)

The axes structure identity, direction and transition. Every decision, module or domain is filtered through these perspectives to prevent drift and maintain coherence.

1.3
Stability & Reproducibility

This layer ensures predictable outcomes, audit‑safety and architectural consistency. Same input → same output. Same structure → same stability.

Layer 2 – Conceptual Domains
Ecosystem Domains

The conceptual domain layer translates the foundation into recognizable ecosystem areas. Only the existence of domains is public — not their internal mapping or validation chains.

2.1
Structural & Architectural Domains

Domains focused on global IT architecture, dependencies and coherence. They safeguard the backbone of the ecosystem.

2.2
Operational & Stability Domains

Domains that focus on operational stability, drift control, incident impact and reproducibility of daily IT operations.

2.3
Human & Governance Domains

Domains that carry the human factor, governance and ecosystem decision‑making. Lightweight structures, maximum stability.

Layer 3 – Modules & Flows
Public Modules

The module‑and‑flow layer makes ecosystem principles tangible through portable modules and controlled flows. This is the most visible layer for teams.

3.1
Ecosystem Modules

Portable components that remain coherent and reproducible within the ecosystem. Examples include framework diagnostic modules, stability checks and ecosystem flow modules.

3.2
Flows & Checks

Ecosystem flows describe how information and decisions move through the landscape. Public checks reveal drift signals or dependency risks without exposing internal chains.

3.3
Public Integration Points

These are the touchpoints where organizations experience MIKE‑CORE: documentation, public modules, diagnostics and reference models.

Public Boundaries
Integrity First

This 3‑layer view intentionally reveals only the upper structure of the ecosystem. The internal blueprint, domain mapping and validation chains remain closed to protect the integrity of MIKE‑CORE.
