Public Modules Overview

A public overview of ecosystemic modules within MIKE‑CORE. This page presents the upper layer of module architecture without exposing internal blueprints, validation chains or domain mappings.

What Are Ecosystemic Modules?

Ecosystemic modules are portable, reproducible components that operate within the MIKE‑CORE ecosystem. They reinforce stability, coherence and drift‑control without depending on frameworks or team structures.

Modules are not scripts, processes or frameworks. They are ecosystemic building blocks that:

  • have a clear identity
  • remain coherent within the whole
  • are reproducible in any context
  • operate drift‑safe
  • make dependencies visible
Public Module Categories

The internal module structure remains closed. What follows is the public upper layer of module categories.

M‑01
Framework Diagnostic Modules

Modules that analyse frameworks for stability, drift, dependencies and coherence. They reveal where frameworks fall short and where ecosystemic correction is required.

M‑02
Stability Check Modules

Modules that measure ecosystemic stability through reproducible checks. They detect drift, inconsistency and dependency breaks.

M‑03
Ecosystemic Flow Modules

Modules that visualise information and decision flows. They show how ecosystemic movement differs from framework‑based flow.

M‑04
Coherence Modules

Modules that verify whether decisions, systems and processes remain coherent within the ecosystemic direction.

M‑05
Identity & Boundary Modules

Modules that safeguard identity, boundaries and role definitions of ecosystemic components.

Public Boundaries

This page reveals only the public upper layer of modules. Internal module mapping, validation chains, TRIAD connections and ecosystemic neural pathways remain closed to protect the integrity of MIKE‑CORE.
