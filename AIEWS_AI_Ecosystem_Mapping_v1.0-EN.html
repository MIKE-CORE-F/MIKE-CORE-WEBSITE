AIEWS • AI Ecosystem Mapping

AI Ecosystem Mapping describes how AIEWS positions itself within the broader MIKE‑CORE ecosystem. Its purpose is to provide a clear, reproducible and audit‑proof view of all AI interactions, dependencies and risk zones.

Why Ecosystem Mapping?

Modern AI systems are no longer isolated tools. They form a network of interconnected dependencies. Without mapping, drift, unpredictability and loss of control emerge.

Core Components of the Mapping
  • Context Layers: how AIEWS monitors context across multiple domains.
  • Risk Zones: points where drift, misinterpretation or instability may arise.
  • Human Intervention Points: where engineers retain final control.
  • Doctrine Protection: how AIEWS prevents expansions from breaking the core.
Relation to TRIAD & MIKE‑CORE

AIEWS functions as a protective layer around TRIAD and MIKE‑CORE. It ensures that all AI interactions remain within ecosystemic boundaries and that every expansion stays reproducible and audit‑proof.
