Training Methodology

The public training approach of MIKE‑CORE: reproducible steps, ecosystemic thinking and stability as the foundation for growth — regardless of background, level or starting position.

This page describes the public training methodology. Internal methods, validation chains and domain structures remain protected.
Why this methodology exists

Training within MIKE‑CORE is not a classic course catalogue. It is an ecosystemic approach designed to protect people from chaos, pressure and unclear expectations. The methodology is built to create stability — not just to transfer knowledge.

Focus
Stability before knowledge

Knowledge without stability breaks people. That is why MIKE‑CORE starts with structure, language and reproducible steps.

Goal
Everyone can enter

The method works for youth, professionals and vulnerable groups — without diploma barriers as a prerequisite.

Approach
Ecosystemic learning

Problems are not viewed in isolation, but in their full context: people, processes, hardware and environment.

The 4 building blocks of MIKE‑CORE training

The training methodology consists of four reproducible building blocks that can be applied in any context.

Block 1
Language & understanding

Participants receive a shared vocabulary: stability, drift, ecosystemic pressure, reproducibility.

Block 2
Structure & safety

A stable environment where mistakes are not an endpoint, but signals in the system.

Block 3
Practical steps

Small, achievable tasks that are reproducible and build confidence over time.

Block 4
Ecosystemic reflection

Not “what did you do wrong?”, but “what in the system caused this behaviour?”.

The MIKE‑CORE training cycle

The training cycle is designed to start small and grow in a reproducible way.

Phase 1 — Foundation
Language & stability

Participants learn the basic concepts and receive a stable environment to start from.

Phase 2 — Application
Small assignments

Practical exercises around hardware, ecosystemic thinking and reproducible steps.

Phase 3 — Reflection
Ecosystemic analysis

Participants learn to look at context, pressure, expectations and system faults instead of blaming individuals.

Phase 4 — Growth
Scaling what works

What works is documented and repeated. Participants grow through stability, not through pressure.
