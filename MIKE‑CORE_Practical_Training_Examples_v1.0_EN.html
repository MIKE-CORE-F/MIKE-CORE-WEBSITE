Practical Training Examples

Concrete, reproducible and ecosystemic: examples of MIKE‑CORE trainings that help youth, professionals and vulnerable groups grow through stability and structure.

This page only shows public examples. Internal methods remain protected.
Why practical examples?

Theory without application stays abstract. MIKE‑CORE works with small, safe, reproducible exercises that give people structure, insight and confidence. These examples show what ecosystemic learning looks like in practice.

Goal
From theory to stability

Every exercise builds stability, not pressure. The focus is on understanding, not performance.

Approach
Small steps

Small assignments that are reproducible and never overwhelming.

Impact
Ecosystemic growth

What works is documented and repeated — for the same person or for others.

Example 1 — Basic Hardware Diagnosis

A simple exercise that teaches youth and beginners how to look at a system without panic or assumptions.

Step 1
Visual inspection

Cables, power, indicator lights, physical damage. No tools, only observation.

Step 2
Context questions

What happened before? Who used it? What changed? Ecosystem instead of blame.

Step 3
Reproducible test

One test at a time. No chaos. Everything can be noted and repeated.

Example 2 — Ecosystemic Pressure Analysis

An exercise that helps professionals avoid sticking problems onto individuals and instead look at the system.

Element 1
Pressure

Which expectations, deadlines or noise influence behaviour?

Element 2
Structure

Which steps are missing? Which roles are unclear?

Element 3
Context

Which external factors play a role? Hardware, environment, communication?

Example 3 — Drift Recognition

An exercise that teaches people to recognise when a system is drifting away from its intended purpose.

Signal 1
Noise

Too much information, too little structure, conflicting expectations.

Signal 2
Overload

Too many tasks, too little time, no priorities.

Signal 3
Loss of reproducibility

Things happen “differently” every time. No predictability anymore.
