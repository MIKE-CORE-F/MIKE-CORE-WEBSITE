Community Impact

How MIKE‑CORE creates ecosystemic value for youth, organisations, local initiatives and vulnerable groups — by combining stability, anti‑waste thinking and socio‑technical growth.

This page only describes the public impact layer.
Why Community Impact?

MIKE‑CORE is not a theoretical framework. It is an ecosystem that creates tangible impact in real environments: schools, neighbourhoods, organisations, youth work and social projects. Impact emerges when stability, structure and anti‑waste thinking come together.

Impact 1
Digital Inclusion

Access to technology, guidance and ecosystemic stability for those who would otherwise be excluded.

Impact 2
Anti‑Waste Culture

Saving, repairing and reusing hardware — with youth, volunteers and local partners.

Impact 3
Ecosystemic Growth

Small projects become reproducible models that can work again in other contexts.

Examples of Impact

MIKE‑CORE creates impact through small, feasible ecosystemic interventions that can later be scaled up.

Project 1
Hardware Rescue

Youth learn to repair, test and document hardware. Less waste, more skills.

Project 2
Digital Basic Skills

Workshops for vulnerable groups: safe internet, e‑mail, documents, digital identity.

Project 3
Ecosystemic Coaching

Professionals learn to recognise drift, reduce noise and work more stably.

Collaborations

MIKE‑CORE collaborates with schools, youth work, local authorities, social organisations and volunteers. Every collaboration starts from stability, reproducibility and respect for vulnerable groups.

Partner 1

Partner 2

Youth receive structure, guidance and access to technology.

Partner 3
Social Organisations

Inclusion projects, hardware rescue and ecosystemic guidance.

Impact Roadmap

Community Impact within MIKE‑CORE grows through a reproducible cycle.

Step 1 — Detection
Making needs visible

Where is stability missing? Where is there digital exclusion? Where does throw‑away thinking appear?

Step 2 — Intervention
Small ecosystemic actions

Saving hardware, giving workshops, coaching, introducing diagnostic structures.

Step 3 — Reproducibility
Document & repeat

What works is documented and applied again in other contexts.

Step 4 — Scaling
Larger projects

Expanding collaborations, reaching new target groups, realising ecosystemic growth.
