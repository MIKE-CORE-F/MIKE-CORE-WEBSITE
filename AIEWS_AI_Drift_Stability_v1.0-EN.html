AIEWS • AI Drift & Stability

Drift & Stability describes how AIEWS detects deviations, maintains stability and prevents AI systems from evolving outside their ecosystem boundaries. The goal is to ensure predictability, safety and reproducibility.

What is AI Drift?

AI Drift occurs when a system diverges from its original context, objectives or doctrine. This can happen due to:

  • misinterpretation of data
  • accumulation of small deviations
  • incorrect feedback loops
  • unintentional human input
  • incorrect module connections

How AIEWS Detects Drift

AIEWS uses context layers, validation points and ecosystem boundaries to detect deviations immediately. It continuously compares:

  • current context vs. original context
  • objectives vs. output
  • module behavior vs. doctrine
  • human input vs. system response

Stability within MIKE‑CORE

Stability means the ecosystem remains predictable, safe and reproducible. AIEWS monitors:

  • ecosystem boundaries
  • module interactions
  • drift‑sensitive zones
  • audit trails
  • human intervention points

Why Drift & Stability Matter

Without drift control, chaos emerges. Without stability, an IT ecosystem eventually collapses. AIEWS prevents both by continuously monitoring, correcting and protecting.
