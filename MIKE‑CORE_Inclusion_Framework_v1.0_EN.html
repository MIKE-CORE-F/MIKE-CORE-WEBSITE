Inclusion Framework

The public inclusion layer of MIKE‑CORE: a socio‑technical structure that protects people, builds stability and actively breaks throw‑away thinking.

This page only describes the public layer. Core structures, domains and validation chains remain protected.
Why this framework exists

Inclusion within MIKE‑CORE is not an “extra”, but a core value. An ecosystem that breaks people is not an ecosystem. The Inclusion Framework makes visible how MIKE‑CORE builds stability for those who fall through classic systems.

Focus
Protecting people

Inclusion does not mean “you may be present”, but being protected from systems that would otherwise break you again.

Goal
Stability as a basic right

Everyone deserves a stable environment to learn, work and grow — regardless of background.

Approach
Ecosystemic view

Problems are not glued onto individuals, but seen as system faults that can be made visible and repairable.

The 3 layers of inclusion

Inclusion within MIKE‑CORE consists of three ecosystemic layers that together form a stable base.

Layer 1
Access

Everyone must be able to enter: without diploma pressure, without fear of failing, without complex barriers.

Layer 2
Understanding

People receive language, structure and context to understand what is happening — and why systems sometimes break.

Layer 3
Growth

Growth does not come from pressure, but from stability, reproducible steps and ecosystemic guidance.

Who MIKE‑CORE protects

The Inclusion Framework focuses on everyone who is written off by classic systems.

Group 1
Youth without diplomas

MIKE‑CORE offers structure, language and safe steps to make growth possible again.

Group 2
Professionals who are stuck

It is not the person who fails, but the system. MIKE‑CORE makes that visible and repairable.

Group 3
Vulnerable groups

People with diagnoses, language barriers, financial problems or gaps in their CV receive stability and protection.

Ecosystemic inclusion cycle

Inclusion within MIKE‑CORE follows a reproducible cycle that can be applied in any context.

Step 1 — Visibility
Making problems visible

The person is not the problem; the pressure, context or missing structure is.

Step 2 — Stability
Building a safe base

People receive structure, language and reproducible steps to regain grip.

Step 3 — Growth
Scaling what works

What works is documented and repeated — without pressure, without chaos.
