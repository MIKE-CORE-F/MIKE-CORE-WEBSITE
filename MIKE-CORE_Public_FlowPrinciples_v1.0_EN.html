MIKE-CORE • Flow Principles

Public Edition • v1.0 • English

1. Purpose of the Flow Principles

The Flow Principles describe how work, information, and responsibility move through the MIKE-CORE ecosystem without creating chaos, overload, or hidden risk. They are written as a public reference so that anyone who touches the ecosystem understands the rules of movement: what may flow, what must not flow, and what must always be traceable.

These principles are not “nice to have” guidelines; they are structural constraints. If a workflow breaks these principles, the ecosystem will eventually pay the price in instability, stress, or loss of trust.

2. Core Flow Principles

2.1 Single Source of Truth

Every critical element (document, module, decision, configuration) has exactly one authoritative home in the ecosystem. Copies may exist for convenience, but they never become the new source of truth.

  • All public MIKE-CORE pages are anchored in one official repository.
  • Updates happen at the source, never in ad‑hoc duplicates.

2.2 Traceable Movement

Anything that moves—files, decisions, responsibilities—must be traceable: where it came from, where it went, and why. Flow without traceability is treated as a risk, not as progress.

  • Every public change can be reconstructed from commits and version history.
  • No “mystery edits” and no undocumented shortcuts.

2.3 No Hidden Load

Flow must never silently increase pressure on people or systems. If a new stream of work is created, the load, ownership, and boundaries must be explicit.

  • New responsibilities are always linked to a clear owner and scope.
  • “Someone will pick this up” is not an acceptable flow pattern.

2.4 Reversible by Design

Any structural change to the ecosystem must be reversible without drama. Flow that cannot be reversed becomes a trap, not a pathway.

  • Directory changes, publishing routes, and naming conventions are designed so they can be rolled back.
  • Emergency rollback is always possible without destroying knowledge.

2.5 Public vs. Internal Boundaries

Flow to the public layer is always deliberate and filtered. Internal blueprints, validation chains, and deep‑structure logic never “accidentally” leak into public space.

  • Public pages expose principles, not raw internal wiring.
  • Anything that could destabilise the ecosystem if misused stays internal by default.

3. Flow in the MIKE-CORE Public Pages

The public HTML pages (Ecosystem Guides, Domain Layers, Modules Overview, Flow Principles) are designed as stable entry points. They are not playgrounds; they are curated doors into the ecosystem.

  • Index page: routes visitors to the correct public documents without exposing internal structure.
  • Each document: has a clear scope and does not duplicate other public content.
  • Links: are stable, human‑readable, and aligned with the file naming conventions.

4. Human‑Safe Flow

Flow is always designed to be human‑safe: no technician, contributor, or reader should be forced into panic, guesswork, or blame because of how the ecosystem is wired.

  • Documentation is written to reduce stress, not increase it.
  • Responsibilities are clear enough that people can say “yes” or “no” without fear.

5. Versioning and Stability

This document is the public Flow Principles reference for MIKE-CORE, version 1.0. Future versions may extend or refine these principles, but they will never break the core intent: stable, traceable, reversible, and human‑safe flow across the ecosystem.
