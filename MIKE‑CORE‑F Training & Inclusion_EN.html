Training & Inclusion

A socio‑technical ecosystem that builds stability for young people, professionals and vulnerable groups — with an explicit stand against throw‑away culture in people, hardware and knowledge.

This page describes the public mission of MIKE‑CORE‑F: how training, inclusion and anti‑waste thinking form a social‑technical layer on top of the technical frameworks.
Why this page exists

MIKE‑CORE‑F is more than an IT ecosystem. It is a structure designed to protect people, create stability and open opportunities for everyone — regardless of diploma, background or starting position. This page makes visible how training, inclusion and anti‑waste culture form a coherent socio‑technical layer.

Focus
People first, systems second

The human being is central, not the tool. MIKE‑CORE‑F uses structure to protect people from chaos, pressure and unnecessary failure experiences.

Goal
Stability as a basic right

Everyone deserves a stable environment to learn, work and grow — including those who fall through classic systems.

Method
Ecosystemic thinking

Problems are not viewed in isolation, but in full context: person, process, hardware, knowledge and environment.

Anti‑Throw‑Away Culture

MIKE‑CORE‑F actively resists throw‑away thinking on three levels: people, hardware and knowledge. Throwing away is always a symptom of a deeper system problem. This ecosystem looks for that cause, makes it visible and builds an alternative.

People
No one is “discarded”

People are not defined by mistakes, diagnoses or diplomas. Stability, structure and guidance make growth possible again.

Hardware
Repair before replacement

Equipment is not replaced by default. Diagnosis, repair and reuse are standard. This reduces cost and protects the environment.

Knowledge
Nothing should be lost

Experience, mistakes and solutions are captured in reproducible structures. No “tribal knowledge”, but an audit‑solid ecosystem.

Reproducible steps
Audit‑solid thinking
1. Training paths for young people without a diploma

Young people often drop out due to abstract theory, unclear expectations and a lack of structure. MIKE‑CORE‑F offers reproducible steps, ecosystemic guidance and a safe learning environment where mistakes are not an endpoint, but a signal in the system.

Structure
Clear diagnostic structures

Instead of “you can’t do it”, the young person gets a clear picture: where does it break, which step is missing, what context is needed?

Guidance
Step‑by‑step stability building

Small, achievable steps rebuild confidence. Each step is reproducible and can be reused later.

Context
Ecosystemic guidance

Not only the young person is considered, but also the environment: home, school, work, digital access, hardware, support network.

Access
Growth without diploma barriers

Talent is not measured in papers, but in stability, effort and learning capacity. MIKE‑CORE‑F opens alternative paths.

2. For professionals who are stuck

A diploma does not guarantee stability. Many professionals face noise, pressure, conflicting expectations and systems that were never designed to protect them. MIKE‑CORE‑F offers an ecosystemic lens to regain control.

Recovery
Restoring stability

By redesigning processes, responsibilities and expectations, space is created again to work in a healthy way.

Awareness
Drift recognition

Professionals learn to recognise when systems drift away from their purpose: too much pressure, too little structure, too much noise.

Analysis
Ecosystemic analysis

Problems are not pinned on individuals, but mapped as system failures. That makes solutions fairer and more sustainable.

Workform
Audit‑solid workflows

Work processes are designed to be reproducible, explainable and defensible — even under pressure.

3. Inclusion for vulnerable groups

MIKE‑CORE‑F protects people who are written off by classic systems: people with a diagnosis, gaps in their CV, financial problems, language barriers or a history of “failure”. Stability is the foundation on which everyone can grow.

Access
Low‑threshold guidance

No requirement of a perfect background. The question is: what is needed to build stability, here and now?

Safety
Ecosystemic safety

People are not only “helped”, but protected against systems that might break them again.

Repeatability
Reproducible steps

What works is captured. So it can work again — for the same person or for someone else.

Growth
Growth for everyone

Inclusion does not mean “being allowed to be present”, but being able to grow in a way that fits the person and the ecosystem.

Possible formats & collaborations

MIKE‑CORE‑F Training & Inclusion is not a classic training catalogue. It is an ecosystem that can collaborate in different forms with schools, organisations, companies and local initiatives.

Introduction
Awareness sessions

Short sessions on throw‑away culture, ecosystemic thinking and stability — for young people, teams or organisations.

Training
Practical tracks

Hands‑on tracks around hardware reuse, digital basic skills, ecosystemic thinking and stability in IT environments.

Projects
Inclusion & anti‑waste projects

Collaborations with schools, social organisations or local initiatives around hardware rescue, training and guidance.

Pilots
Small ecosystemic pilots

Limited pilots to test MIKE‑CORE‑F in a real context: a class, a team, an organisation or a neighbourhood.

Organisations, schools or initiatives that want to explore what is possible can request an exploratory conversation via the public contact channels. Every collaboration starts from stability, reproducibility and respect for vulnerable groups.
Ecosystemic roadmap

Training & Inclusion within MIKE‑CORE‑F does not follow a linear track, but a reproducible cycle. Each step can start small and be scaled later.

Step 1 — Awareness
Making throw‑away culture visible

People, hardware and knowledge are no longer seen as consumables, but as ecosystem elements.

Step 2 — Training
Providing structure and language

The basic concepts of stability, drift, ecosystemic thinking and reproducibility are introduced.

Step 3 — Inclusion project
Concrete application

A small, feasible project in which young people, professionals or vulnerable groups are actively involved.

Step 4 — Ecosystemic growth
Scaling what works

What works is captured, repeated and expanded to other contexts — without losing the core.
